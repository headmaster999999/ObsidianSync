# notes

it seems to me that all films which center children in their own merrymaking just become a newly discovered installment in Francis Alys's Childrens Game series, regardless of whatever it is the director had in mind for the film they "made"... sort of like how wittgenstein boils language down to an essential character as "game", so too are films like squatterpunk essentially just a new iteration of a anterior system of childrens innocence... regardless of the social condition or accompanying elements in the mise en scene, the film and the intentions of the auteur are swept up in pure play. what more is there when it comes to children than their capacity to make fun?  theres very little. a child left to his own devices to play for a camera is enough nourishment for a million films--all of film could be comprised of it. 

all children are auteurs---geniuses in the drama of play.
